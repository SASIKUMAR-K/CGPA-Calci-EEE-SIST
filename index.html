About CGPA and GPA

GPA stands for Grade Point Average, while CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. The typical range for both GPA and CGPA is from 0.0 to 10.0. GPA is calculated for a single semester and represents the average grade points earned during that specific semester only. On the other hand, CGPA is a cumulative measure that considers the overall performance up to a particular semester, including the grades and credits earned in all previous semesters. For example, when calculating the GPA for the third semester, only the marks obtained in courses of the third semester are considered. However, when calculating CGPA for the third semester, marks from all previous semesters (semester 1, semester 2, and semester 3) are taken into account. The formulas for both GPA and CGPA involve calculating the ratio of the sum of all grade points multiplied by the credits to the sum of all credits. It's important to understand what credits are and how to determine them for each course.
